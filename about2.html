.Hardware:

Computer hardware refers to the basic physical components that make up the backbone of modern computing and include everything ranging from the central processing unit that interprets instructions to memory cards and graphics cards. Computer hardware was known to have evolved over the years and shrunk with explosive growth and performance over several decades and is based on the semiconductor technology and the Moore Law that accurately predicted the doubling of the number oftransistors that fit into the microchip every two years. Today's computer hardware is capable of supporting everything ranging from the smartphone that is powered by billions oftransistors to the data centers that drive AI and perform unimaginable operations.

.Networks:

A network is a system that connects computers and devices to be able to share information and resources. Examples include LAN, which stands for Local connection; WAN, meaning Wide reach; Wi-Fi, standing for Wireless link; and Internet, short for Global web. Networks started in the 1960s with simple wired connections and developed into complex systems interconnecting millions of devices all over the world. They enable sharing data, communicating online, working in the cloud, and collaborating remotely. By linking up people and technology, at the core of contemporary work, education, and daily life lies the network.

.ICT's Role in Shaping Modern Life:

  • Communication:Instant messaging, video conferencing, emails, and social media have transformed the way people connect; communication is much faster and more effective.
  • Education:The use of online courses, e-books, and virtual classrooms has expanded access to learning, with a myriad of educational opportunities opening up around the world.
  • Healthcare:Telemedicine, electronic health records, and advanced software for medical treatment enhance the diagnosis, treatment, and quality of patient care.
  • Business and Economy:It increases productivity, reduces operations, and opens new vistas for economic opportunities in e-commerce platforms, digital banking, and online services.
  • Entertainment:Streaming services, online gaming, and digital media offer innovative forms of recreation and cultural engagement.
  • Information Access:Information Access: Knowledge through search engines, news apps, and the like of virtual databases paves one's path towards research and informed decision-making.
  • Government and Services:E-governance initiatives ease administration processes, and make public services access easy, transparent, and effective.
